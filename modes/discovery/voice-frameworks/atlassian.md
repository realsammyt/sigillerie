# Atlassian voice principles

Source: https://www.bigeyeagency.com/insights/ultimate-brand-voice-frameworks-guide (referencing Atlassian's voice and tone foundation)

## Orientation

Atlassian frames voice as a set of principles, not characteristics. More opinionated than Mailchimp. More direct. Where Mailchimp says "fun but not silly," Atlassian says "bold not arrogant" and means it as a rule, not a trait. Tone shifts less between contexts because the brand wants to sound like itself everywhere. Suits B2B SaaS, developer tools, technical products where the audience values credibility over warmth.

## The principles

Four. Each principle has an anti-pattern attached so the rule has teeth.

- **Bold, not arrogant.** Take a position. Pick a side. Do not hedge to look smart. But never punch down at the user, never assume they are slow.
- **Optimistic, not naive.** Believe the work matters. Believe the user can do hard things. But name the trade-offs. Never pretend complexity does not exist.
- **Practical, not boring.** Get to the point. Lead with the thing that matters. Use concrete nouns. But do not flatten every sentence into a memo. Keep some spark.
- **Witty, not silly.** Dry humor is fine. A turn of phrase is fine. Puns and exclamation points are not.

The agent picks all four for most B2B briefs. Drop one only if the vibe-words rule it out (e.g. vibe "grave" rules out witty).

## Application examples

Three product copy snippets in voice. Sigillerie agent generates similar from intake.

| Surface | Copy |
|---|---|
| Hero headline | "Ship the design system. Stop arguing about button radius." |
| Empty state | "No projects yet. Start one. The next move is yours." |
| Error message | "Something broke on our end. We logged it. Try again in a minute." |

Notice: short. Direct. No "Oops!" No exclamation points. The user is treated as a competent adult.

## Tone variation rules

Atlassian leans toward voice mostly constant, tone shifts less than at Mailchimp. The brand sounds like itself in onboarding, in errors, in marketing. Some shift exists:

- Errors get more practical, less witty
- Marketing gets more bold, less practical
- Help docs get more practical, less bold

But the brand is recognizable as one voice across all of them. A user reading three Atlassian surfaces in sequence should feel one author behind all three.

## When this fits

- B2B SaaS and developer tools
- Technical products where credibility comes first
- Enterprise buyers who distrust friendly-startup voice
- Brands whose users are themselves builders, engineers, ops people
- Products where the user pays to skip the small talk

## When NOT to use

- Consumer brands with broad emotional range
- Lifestyle, wellness, fashion, food
- Playful or whimsical products (kids, games, novelty)
- Brands that lean on warmth as a differentiator

The principles feel cold in those contexts. Pick Mailchimp or three-axis instead.
