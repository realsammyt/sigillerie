# Voice card · Vellum

Framework: three-axis (sigillerie/discovery/voice-frameworks/three-axis.md)

## Axis scores

```
formality    -2  -1  [0]  +1  +2
seriousness  -2 [-1]  0   +1  +2
warmth       -2  -1   0  [+1] +2
```

- formality 0, plain, neither street nor press release
- seriousness -1, dry humor, light irony, an occasional turn of phrase
- warmth +1, warm without pushing, glad you're here

These three came from the vibe-words "calm, technical, irreverent". The intake
flinch at performative reading apps pulled warmth up to +1 to keep the brand
human, not clinical. Irreverent landed at -1 seriousness, not -2, because the
audience reads pre-coffee and a wink at +1 hour after waking lands wrong.

## Six example sentences

| Surface     | Sentence                                                           |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Greeting    | "morning. coffee's on you."                                        |
| Success     | "saved. it's in your library now."                                 |
| Error       | "that didn't load. the link looks fine, the server didn't answer." |
| Onboarding  | "pick three papers you'll actually read this week. small numbers." |
| Marketing   | "a reading app for people who'd rather be reading."                |
| Help        | "to mark a paper read: swipe left, or hit done at the end."        |

## Do and don't

| Do                                          | Don't                                                |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Short sentences. Two clauses, often one.    | Stack subordinate clauses. The reader is half awake. |
| Name the actual mechanism.                  | Hedge with "might" when "didn't" is true.            |
| Quiet humor at the seam, not the headline.  | Pun in the page title.                               |
| Lowercase headlines for warmth.             | Title Case Every Surface.                            |
| Talk like one person, not a brand.          | Use "we" when "the team" is meant.                   |
| Trust the reader. Less explanation.         | Re-explain a feature in the same sentence.           |

## Anti-brands, in voice terms

The intake named two adjacent brands as not-Vellum. Voice rules they trigger:

- **bookworm clichés** ("curl up with a good book", reading-as-cozy-hobby)
  → no nouns from that vocabulary. No "curl", no "snuggle", no "tome".
- **gamified reading apps** (streaks, badges, "you read 7 days in a row!")
  → no exclamation points outside genuine errors. No streak language.
  No quantified guilt. The user's reading is theirs, not a leaderboard.

## When in doubt

Read the sentence aloud at 6 AM, before coffee, to a biologist who has not
slept enough. If it sounds like a press release or a self-help book, rewrite.
If it sounds like a friend leaving a note on the kitchen counter, ship.
